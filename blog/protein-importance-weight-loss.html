Why Protein Is the #1 Nutrient for Weight Loss

By Dipti Mistry · November 18, 2024 · 5 min read
💪

When clients come to Krishna Nutrition Center struggling to lose weight despite "eating less," the first thing I check is their protein intake. Nine times out of ten, they're eating mostly roti and rice with very little protein — and that's exactly why the scale won't budge.

Protein isn't just for bodybuilders. It's the single most important nutrient for anyone trying to lose fat, keep muscle, and stay full between meals.

How Protein Helps You Lose Weight

Protein works in three powerful ways:

  • Boosts metabolism — your body burns more calories digesting protein than carbs or fat (called the thermic effect of food)
  • Reduces hunger — protein lowers ghrelin (hunger hormone) and increases feelings of fullness for 3–4 hours
  • Preserves muscle — when you lose weight without enough protein, you lose muscle along with fat, which slows your metabolism long-term

Studies show people on higher-protein diets lose more fat and less muscle compared to low-protein diets — even when total calories are the same.

How Much Protein Do You Need?

A general guideline for weight loss: 0.8–1 gram of protein per kg of body weight daily. So if you weigh 70 kg, aim for 56–70 grams of protein per day.

Most Gujarati vegetarian diets provide only 30–40 grams — far below what's needed. That's where smart planning (and Herbalife supplements) makes a real difference.

Dipti's Tip: One Herbalife Formula 1 shake + Protein Drink Mix gives you 20–25g protein in one meal. That's nearly half your daily target in a single serving.

Best Protein Sources for Gujarati Diets

  • Dal & legumes — moong, masoor, chana (1 katori ≈ 7–9g protein)
  • Paneer — 50g ≈ 10g protein (watch portion size — also high in fat)
  • Eggs — 2 eggs ≈ 12g protein (great breakfast option)
  • Greek yogurt / curd — 1 cup low-fat curd ≈ 8g protein
  • Sprouts — moong sprouts, 1 bowl ≈ 7g protein
  • Herbalife shakes — 17–25g per serving, quick and convenient

Signs You're Not Eating Enough Protein

Watch for these warning signs: constant hunger even after meals, hair thinning, fatigue, slow recovery after exercise, and weight loss that stalls after the first few kilos. If this sounds like you, increasing protein is often the fix — not eating even less food.

Simple Ways to Add More Protein Today

Replace your breakfast with a protein shake. Add a boiled egg or sprouts to lunch. Include dal at both lunch and dinner. Use Herbalife Protein Drink Mix in your evening soup or buttermilk. Small changes, big impact over 30 days.
